The undisputed king of Prismatic Evolutions. The pull rate of approximately 1 in 1,441 packs makes it extraordinarily rare — at $5–6/pack retail, you'd statistically spend over $8,000 trying to pull one. The card peaked near $1,500 in April 2026, up from $882 in February — a 70% gain in six weeks driven by Umbreon's devoted collector base.

It has since settled around $1,200. Umbreon's price floor is structural, not speculative. PSA 10 copies command a significant additional premium.
Verdict: Hold if you own it. Buy the dip. Current $1,200 is below the April peak. Long-term Umbreon demand is one of the most reliable in the hobby.

Is Prismatic Evolutions Sealed Worth Buying?

Honest EV math on every sealed format. The reprint wave changed the calculus significantly — here's what the numbers actually say in June 2026.

Booster Bundle (6 packs)
MSRP: $19.99 · Secondary: $35–$50
⚠ MSRP Only
At $35–$50 secondary you're paying ~$6–8/pack. SIR expected value per pack is roughly $12–$15 but the variance is brutal — most pulls are $0.50–$5. Only buy at MSRP.
Elite Trainer Box (9 packs)
MSRP: $49.99 · Secondary: $80–$120
⚠ MSRP Only
Odds of Umbreon are ~1 in 160 ETBs. Buy at MSRP if you find it. Skip at secondary prices — buy singles instead.
Single Packs (Secondary)
Secondary: $8–$15/pack · MSRP: ~$5.50
❌ Avoid
45–170% premium over MSRP for the thrill of opening. EV is negative at any price above MSRP. Never buy individual packs at secondary prices.
