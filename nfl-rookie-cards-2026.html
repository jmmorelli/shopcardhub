Why 2026 Is Different

The Topps Return Changes Everything

Topps regained the NFL license in April 2026, ending years of Panini monopoly. This matters for collectors: Topps Chrome refractor technology commands premium grading multipliers and deeper long-term liquidity than Panini Prizm. The 2026 draft class is the first class to debut exclusively under Topps — and that scarcity of the "first Topps NFL Chrome" parallels will only grow over time. Working a smaller budget? See our best football cards under $50.

🏈 Key Context: 2026 NFL Draft

Fernando Mendoza (QB, Las Vegas Raiders) — #1 overall pick. Arm talent drawing Joe Burrow comparisons. Pre-draft autos already cleared $400 raw. Carnell Tate (WR, Tennessee Titans) — #4 overall. Projected WR1 with elite route running. His Black Shimmer 1/1 sold for $1,349 pre-Topps Chrome release. Jeremiyah Love (RB, undisclosed) and David Bailey round out the early class movers. Topps Chrome Flagship dropped April 15, 2026 — hobby boxes at ~$360, one auto guaranteed per box.

Fernando Mendoza is the undisputed hobby alpha of the 2026 class. The Raiders traded up to select him #1 overall, and collectors are already drawing Joe Burrow parallels — arm talent, poise, and a franchise QB narrative that drives card prices for years. Pre-Topps Chrome release, raw autos were already climbing past $400, and the print run of 126,501 unlocked Silver, Blue, Gold, Orange, Red, and Superfractor parallels.

The 1/1 Superfractor Auto is the crown jewel — four-figure value floor from day one. For most collectors the Base Chrome Auto is the buy: strong grading premium potential as Topps Chrome refractors historically grade well, and the #1 pick narrative keeps demand durable.

Grading math: If you pull a well-centered raw auto, grading at Economy tier (~$25–$50) can return a meaningful premium. Topps Chrome cards are thick stock with excellent centering tolerance — submit if the surface is clean and corners are sharp.
Verdict: Buy. #1 pick on the first Topps Chrome NFL set in years. Hobby alpha. Pre-draft autos at $400 with room to run on strong Week 1 performance.

Cards to Avoid

2026 NFL Cards to Skip

✗
Off-center Topps Chrome autos at market price — Centering is everything on Chrome. A 60/40 off-center Mendoza raw at $350 is not a deal. It's a PSA 7 waiting to happen. Walk away.
✗
Third-party "collector boxes" or non-Topps products — With Topps holding the exclusive license, unlicensed products lack the team logos and long-term collector demand. Stick to Topps.
✗
Kicker / punter / backup QB cards at premium prices — Draft hype inflates everything short-term. A backup QB auto at $80 raw has a near-zero ceiling. Skip anything outside the top 15 picks unless you have position-specific conviction.
✗
Waiting for "the right time" on Mendoza autos — If he has a strong Week 1, the floor jumps overnight. The best window is now, pre-game action. Post-Week-1 Mendoza will cost more than pre-Week-1 Mendoza. That's historically true for every franchise QB.
