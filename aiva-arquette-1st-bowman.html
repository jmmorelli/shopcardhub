Why Collectors Care
Aiva Arquette is a legitimate freak athlete. At 6-foot-5 and 220 pounds, he's an extreme outlier at shortstop — a position historically dominated by smaller, quicker players. He's also the 2022 Hawaii Gatorade Basketball Player of the Year, a multi-sport background that adds to the athlete narrative collectors love.

At Oregon State in 2025 he hit .354/.461/.654 with 19 home runs and a career-low 16.5% strikeout rate — the production that pushed him to #7 overall. He was a 2025 Golden Spikes Award nominee and replaced Travis Bazzana (#1 overall 2024) at Oregon State, following the exact same path from Corvallis to the first round.

He's currently at Double-A Pensacola after a promotion from High-A Beloit, where he hit .242/.350/.323 with 7 stolen bases in 27 games. The power hasn't fully shown in pro ball yet — which is both the risk and the upside on the card.

What to Skip

Arquette Cards to Pass On

✗
Active BIN listings priced above recent sold comps — The Arquette market is still forming. Some sellers list based on the Orange /25 auction excitement rather than actual sold data. Always filter eBay to completed/sold listings before paying any BIN price.
✗
Non-Chrome base paper cards at elevated prices — The paper base BCP-40 and BTP-18 are worth $2–$5. If you see them priced at $15–$25 due to name recognition, the Chrome version is a far better investment at the same price point.
✗
Redemption cards with unrealistic PSA grading expectations — Because CPA-AA is a redemption, you won't see the actual card before it ships. Don't send a fulfilled redemption to PSA without first inspecting it for centering and surface issues — redemptions can vary in print quality.
✗
Betting heavily on the power profile before pro data shows it — The college HR output is real. The pro power hasn't translated yet. Buying one or two key cards is smart; stacking multiples of the numbered parallels before he hits Double-A homers is an aggressive speculative position.
✗
Third-party graded copies without verifying the pop report first — The 2026 Bowman grading rush is underway. Check PSA's pop report before paying a PSA 10 premium — high populations compress the premium significantly for some 2026 Bowman cards.
